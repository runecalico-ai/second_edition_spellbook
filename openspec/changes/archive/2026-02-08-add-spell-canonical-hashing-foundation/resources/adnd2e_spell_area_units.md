
# AD&D 2nd Edition – Canonical Units of Area for Priest & Wizard Spells

This document enumerates **every unit of area measurement used to define Areas of Effect**
in **AD&D 2nd Edition** priest and wizard spells, aggregated across **all TSR-era sourcebooks**,
including but not limited to:

- Player’s Handbook / Dungeon Master Guide
- Tome of Magic
- Spell Compendiums Vol. 1–4
- Complete Priest’s Handbook
- Complete Wizard’s Handbook
- Player’s Option (Spells & Magic)
- Forgotten Realms, Greyhawk, Dark Sun, Planescape, Ravenloft
- Specialty priest and high-level magic supplements

This list captures **measurement units**, not spell shapes alone.

---

## 1. Linear Distance Units (Used to Define Area)

- Foot / Feet (ft.)
- Yard / Yards (yd.)
- Mile / Miles
- Inch / Inches (in.)

### Level-Scaled Linear Units
- Feet per level
- Yards per level
- Miles per level

---

## 2. Radial Measurements

### Circular / Planar
- Radius (feet or yards)
- Diameter (rare, explicit)

### Spherical / Volumetric
- Radius sphere
- Diameter sphere (rare)

---

## 3. Rectilinear & Dimensional Units

- Length
- Width
- Height
- Thickness

Typically combined to form walls, boxes, prisms, or barriers.

---

## 4. Cones & Angular Areas

- Cone (length-based)
- Fan-shaped area
- Angle-defined cone (degrees, implicit)

---

## 5. Linear Effects

- Line (length × width)
- Beam
- Ray
- Path-based (along road, river, or route)

---

## 6. Cubic & Volumetric Units

### Cubes
- Cube (feet)
- Cube (yards)

### Volumes
- Cubic feet
- Cubic yards

Used primarily for clouds, fogs, vapors, and fills.

---

## 7. Cylinders & Columns

- Cylinder (radius + height)
- Column (width + height)

Common in weather, fire, and divine effects.

---

## 8. Surface-Based Units

- Square feet
- Square yards
- Squares (10-ft. squares)
- Hexes (outdoor scale)
- Rooms
- Floors / dungeon levels

- One creature
- One creature per level
- All creatures within area

- Allies / enemies within area

## 14. Mobile, Dynamic, or Conditional Areas

- Mobile radius (moves with caster)
- Drifting area
- Expanding radius
- Shrinking area
- Anchored area

---

## Canonical Rules Note

AD&D 2nd Edition **does not use a single unified geometric area system**.
Spell areas are intentionally defined using **natural language, geometry,
environmental context, and metaphysical boundaries**, relying on DM adjudication.

This list represents **every area unit ever used**, not an abstraction.
